# TASTE.md — craft guidance (worked examples, never gates)

How to make an edit *good*. Everything here is a **worked example and a default**, not
a mandate — the footage and the style decide. Hard rules (correctness) are in
`RULES.md`; if something can be mechanically checked, it belongs there, not here.

> Distilled from `knowledge/edit-grammar.md` §A/§C/§D and `knowledge/craft-reference.md`
> (both stay in `knowledge/` as the fuller source until this file fully absorbs them,
> then they move to `legacy/`). The editor and director LLM both read this.

## Rhythm & scale

- **Scale = distance to the viewer (the ladder).** Wider reads as context/calm, tighter
  reads as intimacy/intensity. Move *along* the ladder deliberately; a video has a
  scale arc, not random framings. Pick each shot's rung for what the moment means.
- **One tool per transition, chosen on purpose.** Cut, punch-in, bridge — each says
  something different. Match the tool to the intent of the boundary (§A2).
- **Hook framing is a decision, not a default.** The opener's scale/energy is chosen to
  stop the scroll for *this* content — not inherited from the template.
- **Tempo = speech tempo with the dead air removed.** Don't manufacture punchiness the
  footage doesn't have (that's RULES D5); pace comes from tightening gaps, not from
  fake cuts.

## Emphasis & recapture

- **Photo-snap** (freeze/still punch) is the house attention-recapture tool — powerful,
  so ~1× per video max. Overuse spends the effect.
- **Preserve the peaks.** Laughs, the turn in a story, the payoff line — extend through
  the reaction; the beat *is* the payoff, don't clip it early.

## B-roll (craft side; the mechanics are RULES §B)

- **Illustrate the problem and the insight; keep the offer on her face** (§C1). B-roll
  carries the pain and the reframe; the CTA is human and direct.
- **A named behaviour wants to be shown** — overlap the B-roll with the words, don't
  wait for the sentence to finish (§C2).
- **The sentence is the instruction** (§C3): extract the claim (subject, count/scale,
  valence, claim-type) *first*, then find the moment that answers it. "100,000 owners"
  is plural → show plurality. This reasoning is mandatory (RULES D7); the *aptness* of
  the choice is taste.
- **One B-roll style per video** (§C4) — fullscreen or PiP as the style dictates, not
  mixed ad-hoc.
- **Spread it, leave air** (§C6). Don't clump inserts; let the face breathe between them.
- **Overlay B-roll** (§C7): when she should keep talking *and* the dog should be seen,
  the PiP/overlay layer carries the dog as a second attention channel.

## Captions & end-card

- **Captions yield to the image** (§D1) — when B-roll needs the frame, captions get out
  of the way; they serve the picture, not vice-versa.
- **End-card lives in the safe area, click-instruction held to the end** (§D2). Geometry
  is a hard rule (B9); the styling and wording are taste.

## The director's eye (both checkpoints)

Reading a plan (7b) or a render (step 9), the question is always the felt one: does the
hook land, does the arc hold, does the rhythm breathe, is each B-roll the *right* image
and not merely a tag-match. Code proves it's correct; taste decides if it's *good*.
